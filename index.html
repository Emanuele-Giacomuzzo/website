About me

Hi! I'm Ema, and I study how global change affects the connectivity among ecosystems, with cascading effects on ecosystem function and biodiversity. I'm currently a postdoc at the University of Copenhagen in the Freshwater Biology Section working with Jana Isanta-Navarro and Kirsten Seestern Christoffersen. I recently (October 2025) graduated my PhD with Florian Altermatt at the Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology (Eawag) and University of Zurich in Switzerland.

Ecosystems are connected to each other. One of the ways by which ecosystems can be connected is via the flow of resources among them. For example, leaves fall from forest trees into streams, bears transport fish from rivers deep into forests where they leave fish scraps, and wind carries nutrients across the Atlantic to link the Sahara Desert with the Amazon rainforest. However, despite the growing recognition of the ubiquity and importance of these connections, we still do not understand how global changes affect biodiversity and ecosystem function via changes in these connections. Therefore, my research studies what the impacts of different dimensions of global change are affecting ecological ecosystems via the mediating role of connectivity via resource flows. During my PhD I studied how changes in ecosystem size can affect the effects of resource flows, while during my Postdoc I study how the rise in cyanobacterial blooms can affect the export of nutrients from aquatic systems via emerging insects.
