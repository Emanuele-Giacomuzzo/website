About Me

Hi! I'm Ema, and I study how global change affects the connectivity among ecosystems, with cascading effects on ecosystem function and biodiversity. I'm currently a postdoc at the University of Copenhagen in the Freshwater Biology Section working with Jana Isanta-Navarro and Kirsten Seestern Christoffersen in Denmark. I recently (October 2025) graduated my PhD with Florian Altermatt at the Swiss Federal Institute of Aquatic Science and Technology (Eawag, ETH domain) and the University of Z√ºrich in Switzerland.

Ecosystems are connected via the flow of resources. For example, leaves fall from forest trees into streams, bears transport fish from rivers deep into forests where they leave fish scraps, and wind carries nutrients across the Atlantic to link the Sahara Desert with the Amazon rainforest. However, despite the growing recognition of the importance of these connections, we generally don't understand how global change affects them, causing cascading effects on biodiversity and ecosystem function. My research focuses on this gap. During my PhD, I studied how changes in ecosystem size can affect the effects of resource flows, while during my Postdoc I study how the rise in cyanobacterial blooms can affect the export of nutrients from aquatic systems via emerging insects.
